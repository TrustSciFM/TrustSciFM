Paving the Way for Trustworthy Science Foundation Models: Challenges and Frontiers

@ NeurIPS 2025

The first dedicated workshop to tackle the trustworthiness crisis in Scientific Foundation Models, uniting experts from diverse scientific domains to confront urgent concerns about reliability, evaluation frameworks, and accountability mechanisms.

Submit Paper Learn More

About the Workshop

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is no longer just an auxiliary tool in scientific research—it is rapidly becoming a fundamental driver of discovery. Scientific Foundation Models (SciFMs) have emerged as transformative instruments across biology, chemistry, physics, and medicine, offering unprecedented potential to accelerate breakthroughs.

However, their integration into science is not without critical risks. SciFMs suffer from alarming trustworthiness issues, including scientific hallucinations, biases, reproducibility failures, and opacity in decision-making. If left unaddressed, these challenges could severely undermine scientific integrity and stall AI-driven progress.

Key Expected Outcome

A core deliverable of this workshop will be a collaborative white paper, serving as a definitive resource for guiding the future of SciFM research and development.

The workshop will support virtual participation via live-streamed sessions and an interactive platform. Remote attendees can join Q&A, engage in panel discussions, and explore posters. Session recordings will be available post-event.

Call for Papers

To foster interdisciplinary research on SciFMs, we invite authors from all disciplines (ML, NLP, CV, Data Mining, Chemistry, Biology, Physics, Social Sciences). This workshop will explore both the development of novel models and the trustworthiness of existing ones.

Paper Tracks

Main Paper Track

Full research papers presenting novel contributions

Position Paper Track

Opinion pieces and perspective papers

Demo Paper Track

System demonstrations and practical applications

Topics of Interest

Model Innovation and Training

Design of novel SciFM architectures, domain-adaptive pretraining techniques, and advancements in transfer learning

Evaluation and Validation

Development of benchmarks, metrics, and reproducibility frameworks for robust performance

Bias, Fairness, and Ethics

Approaches to detect and mitigate dataset and model biases, address equity across disciplines

Explainability and Interpretability

Tools and frameworks to improve model transparency and understanding of decision-making processes

Applications and Impact

Demonstrations of SciFM applications in real-world scenarios and analysis of their impacts

Collaboration and Open Science

Strategies to promote interdisciplinary research and democratize access to SciFMs
